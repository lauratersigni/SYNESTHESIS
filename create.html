create your own
In order to create your own, you must taste a piece of food either a single food item such as an apple or an entire meal for instance having an appetizer and then a main dish. After tasting the food use the shapes below to create your own translation of food. Each shape is defined with a key descriptive word. Could you create a narrative of your experience by using these shapes? Start by thinking of the beginning bites and how they tasted compared to the last ones.

Depending on your taste buds try to translate what you have tasted using these shapes. To start creating your own visual translation use your cursor click the shape you desire to use. Use the grey box as your digital canvas. The shape you have clicked will appear at the top corner of the grey box. You may place this shape anywhere in the grey box by clicking the object and dragging it where you want. You may expand the size of the shapes by clicking and dragging the corner of the shape.

Taste Legend

Sweet
Adjective
Having the taste or flavour characteristic of sugar, honey, etc.

Sour
Adjective
Having an acid taste, resembling that of vinegar, lemon juice, etc., tart


Fresh
Adjective
Foods that contain lots of water and tasting weak. Foods that absorbs water. Most common in fruits.

Salty
Adjective
Tasting of or containing salt; saline.


Bitter
Adjective
Having a harsh, disagreeably acrid taste, like that of aspirin, quinine, wormwood, or aloes.

Texture
Adjective
Also known as mouthfeel. Refers to the texture and physical feeling you get from what you eat.
