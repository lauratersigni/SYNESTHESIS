Our sense of sight always informs the experiences that connect and evoke feeling, regardless of the medium. This is often seen in graphic design where the senses of sight reigns supreme, leaving the four senses to an afterthought. Therefore, I want to look at how graphic design could engage in more than one sense. If I am producing works using more than one sense will this cause for a synesthetic experience? How will those with synesthesia perceive my designs?

As part of my thesis I began to think about how taste would look like visually and how it would sound if it had a noise. I wanted to create a synesthetic experience for those who do not have synesthesia. As I began to develop what form this project would take a large part of my thinking was to create an artefact that truly keeps in mind more than one senses when designing. As a designer I enjoy developing and designing interactive pieces that could reach a wide audience. Therefore, I asked my fellow classmates to participate in my taste experiment. The experiment is as follows the participants are asked to choose a food item that is placed on the table. To document their experience of taste I took photos of the participant eating the chosen food. While, they are eating the foods the participant and I are discussing what they are tasting through descriptive words. Once they are finished they are asked to visually translate what they experienced using guidelines provided by me. You may view these guidelines and discover more about this experiment on the taste page. Below you may view pictures from the experiment I conducted. If you hover over the pictures with your cursor you will be able to view that persons visual creation with the particular food.
